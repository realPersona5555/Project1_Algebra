Linear Equations

Linear Equation
– An equation when graphed out, forms a straight line. Linear Equations don’t have exponents, square, cube roots or anything of the like.

Common Forms

Slope-Intercept Form | y = mx + b

As the name suggests, this form makes use of the slope of the line and its y-intercept.

  • m - slope
  • b - intercept


Point-Slope Form | y – y1 = m(x – x1)

This form uses 2 points on the line and it's slope for it's equation.

  • x,y - coordinates of a point
  • x1,y1 - coordinates of another point
  • m - slope


General Form | Ax + By + C = 0

  • A and B cannot be 0 at the same time
  • x and y are the x and y-intercepts respectively

Solving Linear Equations

Similar to our previous lesson on balancing expressions, to solve a linear equation, you must first isolate the unknown variable then simplify everything else on the other side. Remember to always follow the Order of Operations when simplifying.

To solve for the value of an unknown variable in a linear equation:

  • Isolate the variable
  • Simplify

Examples

Find the value of y in the following equation: 6(y + 1) = 12

First, let's divide both sides by 6 to remove the 6 on the left side.
y + 1 = 2

Now we subtract 1 from both sides to remove the +1 on the left side.
y = 2 – 1
y = 1

Finding Intercepts and Slopes

Intercept
- The point wherein the drawn lines intersects the y or x axis on a cartesian plane.

Slope
- Determines the steepness of the line or how slant it is.

To be able to graph linear equations, you must be able to identify at which point the line comes in contact with the x or y axis(intercept) and how steep it is(slope).

The Slope-Intercept Form is the most common and simplest form for writing linear equations in.

y = mx + b

  • m is the slope
  • b is the intercept

By simply following the basic structure of the equation, you can easily pick out the slope and y-intercept.

If the slope is positive, the line goes upwards going left to right.
If the slope is negative, the line goes downwards going left to right.
The y-intercept can be negative as well. If it is, it just means that the point where it makes contact with the y-axis is below the origin point (0,0)

Examples

  • In the equation y = 6x + 10, the slope is 6 and the y-intercept is 10.
  • In the equation y = -9x + 13, the slope is -9 and the y-intercept is 13.
  • In the equation y = -6x - 21, the slope is -6 and the y-intercept is -21.

Test Yourself!

Identify the slopes and y-intercepts of the following linear equations:

  • -y = -5x + 16
  • y = -7 + 2x
  • y = 4x - 12

Identify the value of x for the following linear equations:

  • 48 ÷ 4 + 2x = 86
  • 30 – 18 + 12x = 128
  • 21 ÷ (6 ÷ 2) = x + 3

Answers (Hover to Show)

  • m = 5 | b = -16
  • m = 2 | b = -7
  • m = 4 | b = -12
  • x = 37
  • x = 9
  • x = 4

Recap

Linear equations are equations that when plotted on a grraph, draw a straight line. Its common forms make the use of slopes, points and intercepts on the straight line to come up with an equation to graph out that line.
