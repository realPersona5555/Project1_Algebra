Did You Know?

Balancing expressions is such an important part of algebra that it's been around since the first algebra book ever made! Specifically, it was first introduced by the Persian mathematician al-Khwarizmi in his famous book al-Jabr, which is also where algebra got its name from. The book gives instructions on how to simplify or 'reduce' expressions, then 'balance' them out so they could be solved.

Balancing Expressions

Balanced Equation – An equation where both sides have the same value.

To balanced an unbalanced equation:

  • Solve for both sides of the equation
  • Add what’s missing to make the equation equal on both sides

Examples

3 × 2 = 5 is unbalanced and false
3 × 2 = 5 + 1 is balanced and true

56 – 20 = 72 is unbalanced and false
56 – 20 = 72 ÷ 2 is balanced and true

This can apply to when you solve for the values of variables.

To solve for the value of an unknown variable:

  • Isolate the variable
  • Simplify

Let’s try solving for x in the following equation: 5x + 9 = 34

To isolate the variable, we must subtract 9 from the left side of the equation. Subtracting it from just the left side it isn’t allowed since it will make the equation unbalanced. If you perform one operation on one side of an equation, you must do the same to the other side.

Let’s subtract 9 from both sides:
5x + 9 – 9 = 34 – 9

From there we can simplify the equation into:
5x = 25

We can deduce that 5 x’s results into 25 but what about 1 x?
Let’s divide both sides by 5 to get the value for 1 x or just simply x:
5x ÷ 5 = 25 ÷ 5
x = 5
x is therefore equal to 5.

Let’s check. Replace all x’s with 5’s in the original equation:
5x + 9 = 34
5(5)+9 = 34
25 + 9 = 34
34 = 34

The equation is balanced, and the value of x has been solved for.

Test Yourself!

Find the value of x in the following equations:

  • 3x − 4 = 2x
  • 2x + 3 = x + 6
  • 5x + 2 = 4x + 4

Answers (Hover to Show)

  • x = 4
  • x = 3
  • x = 2

Recap

To recap, we balance algebraic expressions by applying the same operations on both sides of the equation. If the right side is multiplied by 2, then the left side must be multiplied by 2 as well.

Can we try one of the example problems above and see how balancing lets us solve for x? I don't really get it...

Sure! Let's use Problem 1: 3x - 4 = 2x.

  • 3x - 4 = 2x
  • 3x - 4 + 4 = 2x + 4
  • 3x = 2x + 4
  • 3x - 2x = 2x - 2x + 4
  • x = 4

Oh, I see how balancing works now! Eventually, we should end up with the variable isolated on one side!
