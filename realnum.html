Did You Know?

Back in Ancient Greece, some people believed real numbers were a big deal! The ancient Greek mathematician Pythagoras and his students believed that the Universe was made of rational numbers. So, they were quite shocked when Hippasus, himself one of Pythagoras' students, proved that √2 is in fact irrational! Some claim that he was punished by the gods for revealing this knowledge when he drowned at sea.

Real Numbers

Real Numbers
– Any number that can be plotted onto the real number line. It can either be rational or irrational.

Real Number Line
– A line that contains all possible real numbers. The values of the numbers as you go right increases and the values of the numbers as you go left decrease until you eventually reach negative numbers.

Classification of Real Numbers

Irrational Numbers
– Numbers that cannot be expressed as a fraction or quotient of 2 integers. Any non-rational number is automatically an irrational number.

– Pi and most square roots and cube roots are irrational. √2 is proved to be irrational by Hippasus, one of Pythagorean's students.

Rational Numbers
– Fractions containing integers wherein the denominator is not 0. We can say that all natural numbers, whole numbers, and integers are rational numbers with their denominator being 1. Since fractions can be written as decimals, decimals weather terminating or repeating are also considered rational numbers.

Integers
– Contains negative and positive numbers as well as 0. Reffered to as positive/negative/nonzero integers.

Whole Numbers
– Contains natural numbers as well as 0. Numbers with no decimal points.

Natural Numbers
– Also known as counting numbers. 0 is not a natural number.

Examples

  • -8 is a negative integer and is considered a rational number.
  • 0.78 is a rational number but not an integer, whole or natural number.
  • 0 is not a natural number but is considered a whole number.

Recap

Real numbers are basically any number than can be plotted on the number line. A majority of the numbers we use in math are real numbers, which means that they are also divided into smaller subsets so we can categorize them better.

Starting from the innermost, smallest classification, the natural numbers, also known as counting numbers, are numbers that you can use to count objects. Examples of counting numbers are 1, 2, 3, 4, etc.

Whole numbers include all the counting numbers and 0. 0 is not a counting number, but it is a whole number. All counting numbers are whole numbers as well. In short, whole numbers are counting numbers, but with a "hole" (0) in it.

Integers include all the positive whole numbers as well as the negative whole numbers such as -1, -2, -3, etc. The only thing you need to remember is that a number is an integer if it does not have a decimal/fraction attached.

Rational numbers are used to define all numbers that can be expressed in fraction form. This includes all the integers, but also decimals and fractions such as 3.75 and 6/7.

Irrational numbers are the exact opposite of rational numbers, as you may have guessed; this is because they cannot be expressed in fraction form at all. An irrational number is easy to spot in decimal form; it is a decimal that does not end and does not repeat a sequence of digits.

Finally, the real numbers are the combination of both the rational and irrational numbers. If something is a rational number, it is a real number as well. If something is an irrational number, it is also a real number.
